# Pulse Recovery Test Plan — iPhone + Apple Watch

Thanks for helping test Pulse Recovery. The aim is to check whether the app is easy to install, whether the watch session recording works, and whether the recovery feedback is clear and useful.

## 1. Install the iPhone app

1. Open the TestFlight invite link.
2. Install Pulse Recovery on your iPhone.
3. Open the app.
4. Register a new account or sign in.

Please check whether the login/register process is clear.

## 2. Install the Apple Watch app

After installing the iPhone app:

1. Open the Watch app on your iPhone.
2. Go to **My Watch**.
3. Scroll to **Available Apps** or find **Pulse Recovery**.
4. Install it on your Apple Watch if it is not already installed.
5. Open Pulse Recovery on the Apple Watch.

Please report if the watch app does not appear or will not install.

## 3. Record a recovery session on Apple Watch

After a workout or a short effort that raises your heart rate:

1. Open Pulse Recovery on the Apple Watch.
2. Press the Start Workout button before your workout
3. At the end of your workout start a recovery recording by pressing the End Workout button
4. Keep your watch on your wrist during the recovery period (2 minutes)
5. Stay still or walk gently during recovery.
6. Keep the watch snug on your wrist.
7. Let the recording complete.
8. Keep the watch and iPhone near each other so the session can sync.

The app is designed to record heart rate recovery after exercise. It is fine to test with a light effort, but a clearer test usually happens after your heart rate has risen noticeably.

## 4. Import the watch session on iPhone

On the iPhone app:

1. Go to **New Assessment**.
2. Look for a recent watch session.
3. Tap **Use Watch Session**.
4. Check that peak HR, 60-second HR, and 120-second HR are filled in.
5. Add:

   * How hard the workout felt, from 1–10
   * How you felt afterwards, from 1–10
6. Tap **Generate Assessment**.

Please check whether the watch session is easy to find and whether the filled-in values look sensible.

## 5. Review the result

After generating the assessment, check:

* The recovery percentage
* The recovery classification
* The recovery curve or pattern explanation
* The advice/coaching message
* Whether the result feels clear and useful

Please report anything that feels repetitive, too technical, confusing, or misleading.

## 6. Check the history page

Go to **History Log** and check:

* The new assessment appears
* The date and values look right
* The recovery curve displays correctly
* The summary is understandable

Try creating more than one assessment if possible, so the app can start showing comparisons over time.

## 7. Optional manual test

Also try creating one assessment manually by entering:

* Peak heart rate
* 60-second heart rate
* 120-second heart rate
* Effort score
* Feeling-after score

This helps check that the app still works even without a watch session.

## 8. What to report

Please send:

* Screenshots of anything confusing or broken
* Your iPhone model and iOS version
* Your Apple Watch model and watchOS version, if using the watch app
* Whether the watch session synced successfully
* Whether the recovery advice felt useful

Pulse Recovery provides general fitness and recovery feedback only. It is not intended to diagnose medical conditions or replace medical advice.
